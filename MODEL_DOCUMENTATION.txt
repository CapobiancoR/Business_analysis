AI FINANCE PLATFORM - FINANCIAL MODEL v7 DOCUMENTATION
================================================================================

This document provides a comprehensive explanation of all variables, parameters,
and formulas used in the financial model. It covers:
1. All assumptions (input parameters)
2. Monthly model calculations (36 months across 3 years)
3. Yearly summary aggregations
4. The logic behind each formula


================================================================================
SECTION 1: ASSUMPTIONS (INPUT PARAMETERS)
================================================================================

These are the configurable parameters that drive the entire model. They are
defined for each year (Year 1, Year 2, Year 3) and can be adjusted to simulate
different business scenarios.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1.1 REVENUE PARAMETERS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ARPU (Average Revenue Per User)
  • Unit: EUR / month / user
  • Default: 20 EUR
  • Description: Average monthly revenue generated per paying subscriber.
    This represents the typical price point across all pricing tiers.
  • Usage: Used to calculate MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue)
  • Formula where used: MRR = Paying_Users_End × ARPU

GrossMargin
  • Unit: Decimal (0-1)
  • Default: 0.80 (80%)
  • Description: Typical SaaS gross margin, representing the percentage of
    revenue retained after direct costs (e.g., hosting, infrastructure).
    70-90% is standard for SaaS businesses.
  • Usage: Used in LTV (Lifetime Value) calculation
  • Formula where used: LTV = (ARPU × GrossMargin) / Churn_Rate

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1.2 CONVERSION PARAMETERS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ConvVS (Conversion: Visitor to Signup)
  • Unit: Decimal (0-1)
  • Default: 0.13 (13%)
  • Description: Percentage of website visitors who sign up for the platform.
    This represents the top-of-funnel conversion rate.
  • Usage: Converts total visitors into signups
  • Formula where used: Signups = Visitors_Total × ConvVS

ConvSP (Conversion: Signup to Paying)
  • Unit: Decimal (0-1)
  • Default: 0.035 (3.5%)
  • Description: Percentage of signups who convert to paying subscribers.
    This represents the bottom-of-funnel conversion rate.
  • Usage: Converts signups into paying users (by channel)
  • Formula where used: 
    - Org_New_Payers = Org_Signups × ConvSP
    - Inf_New_Payers = Inf_Signups × ConvSP
    - Other_New_Payers = Other_Signups × ConvSP

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1.3 SOCIAL MEDIA GROWTH PARAMETERS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Followers_0 (Initial Followers)
  • Unit: Count
  • Default: 1000
  • Description: Starting number of social media followers at the beginning
    of the model (Month 1, Year 1).
  • Usage: Initial value for follower growth calculations

Follower_Monthly_Growth
  • Unit: Decimal (0-1)
  • Default: 0.08 (8% per month)
  • Description: Compound monthly growth rate of social media followers.
    Followers grow organically through content and engagement.
  • Formula where used: Followers_End = Followers_Start × (1 + Follower_Monthly_Growth)

Posts_per_Month_Y1, Posts_per_Month_Y2, Posts_per_Month_Y3
  • Unit: Count
  • Default: 120 posts/month (all years)
  • Description: Number of social media posts published per month in each year.
    Higher posting frequency increases reach and engagement.
  • Usage: Determines total impressions generated

Reach_per_Post
  • Unit: Decimal (0-1)
  • Default: 0.04 (4%)
  • Description: Percentage of followers who see each individual post.
    Platform algorithms determine organic reach per post.
  • Usage: Calculates impressions to followers

NonFollower_Reach_Multiplier
  • Unit: Decimal (0-1)
  • Default: 0.5 (50%)
  • Description: Social platforms show posts to non-followers through
    explore pages, hashtags, and recommendations. This multiplier determines
    how many non-follower impressions are generated relative to follower
    impressions.
  • Formula where used: Impr_NonFollowers = Impr_Followers × NonFollower_Reach_Multiplier

Frequency_Impressions_per_User
  • Unit: Number
  • Default: 1.5
  • Description: Average number of times the same user sees your content
    in a month (frequency). Users may see multiple posts or the same post
    multiple times.
  • Usage: Converts impressions into unique users reached

Organic_CTR_to_Site (Organic Click-Through Rate)
  • Unit: Decimal (0-1)
  • Default: 0.015 (1.5%)
  • Description: Percentage of unique non-followers who click through to
    the website from social media posts.
  • Formula where used: Org_Visitors = NewUnique_NonFollowers × Organic_CTR_to_Site

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1.4 CHURN PARAMETERS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ChurnY1, ChurnY2, ChurnY3 (Monthly Churn Rate per Year)
  • Unit: Decimal (0-1)
  • Defaults: 0.06 (6%), 0.055 (5.5%), 0.05 (5%)
  • Description: Monthly churn rate - percentage of paying users who cancel
    their subscription each month. Typically improves over time as product
    matures and customer success improves.
  • Formula where used: Churned_Users = Paying_Users_Start × Churn_Rate

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1.5 INFLUENCER MARKETING PARAMETERS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Inf_Avg_Followers
  • Unit: Count
  • Default: 1,000,000
  • Description: Average follower count of influencers you collaborate with.

Inf_Reach_Rate
  • Unit: Decimal (0-1)
  • Default: 0.3 (30%)
  • Description: Percentage of influencer's followers who see the sponsored post.

Inf_Click_Rate
  • Unit: Decimal (0-1)
  • Default: 0.02 (2%)
  • Description: Percentage of users who see the influencer post and click
    through to your website.

Inf_Visitors_per_Collab (Influencer Visitors per Collaboration)
  • Unit: Count
  • Default: 6000
  • Description: Calculated value: Inf_Avg_Followers × Inf_Reach_Rate × Inf_Click_Rate
    Represents website visitors generated per influencer collaboration.

Inf_Collabs_Y1, Inf_Collabs_Y2, Inf_Collabs_Y3 (Influencer Collaborations per Year)
  • Unit: Count per month
  • Defaults: 1, 1, 1 (1 collaboration per month)
  • Description: Number of influencer collaborations executed per month in each year.
  • Formula where used: Inf_Visitors = Inf_Collabs × Inf_Visitors_per_Collab

Influencer_Reward_per_Sub
  • Unit: EUR
  • Default: 10 EUR
  • Description: Commission paid to influencers for each new paying subscriber
    they generate (performance-based compensation).
  • Formula where used: Inf_Marketing_Spend = Inf_New_Payers × Influencer_Reward_per_Sub

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1.6 REFERRAL PROGRAM PARAMETERS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Referral_Monthly_Rate
  • Unit: Decimal (0-1)
  • Default: 0.02 (2%)
  • Description: Monthly referral rate - percentage of existing paying users
    who successfully refer a new paying customer each month.
  • Formula where used: Referral_New_Payers = Paying_Users_Start × Referral_Monthly_Rate

Referral_Reward_per_Sub
  • Unit: EUR
  • Default: 10 EUR
  • Description: Reward paid to existing users for each successful referral
    (e.g., account credit, cash reward).
  • Formula where used: Referral_Marketing_Spend = Referral_New_Payers × Referral_Reward_per_Sub

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1.7 OTHER MARKETING PARAMETERS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Org_Cost_per_Post (Organic Social Cost per Post)
  • Unit: EUR
  • Default: 1 EUR
  • Description: Cost to create and publish each organic social media post
    (content creation, design, management).
  • Formula where used: Org_Marketing_Spend = Posts × Org_Cost_per_Post

Other_Marketing_Budget_Y1, Other_Marketing_Budget_Y2, Other_Marketing_Budget_Y3
  • Unit: EUR per month
  • Defaults: 200, 400, 600 EUR
  • Description: Monthly budget for other marketing channels (e.g., paid ads,
    SEO, content marketing, partnerships).
  • Formula where used: Other_Marketing_Spend = Other_Marketing_Budget (per year)
  • Also used: Other_Visitors = Other_Marketing_Budget / 2.0 (assumes $2 CPC)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1.8 COST PARAMETERS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

BaseFixedCost
  • Unit: EUR per month
  • Default: 1000 EUR
  • Description: Fixed monthly operational costs (salaries, office, software,
    legal, accounting, etc.).
  • Formula where used: Total_Costs includes Base_Fixed_Cost

DataSub_Fee
  • Unit: EUR per month
  • Default: 2000 EUR
  • Description: Monthly subscription fee for data provider service.

DataSub_MRR_Threshold
  • Unit: EUR
  • Default: 5000 EUR
  • Description: MRR threshold above which the data subscription is activated.
    Below this threshold, the cost is 0.
  • Formula where used: DataSub_Cost = DataSub_Fee if MRR ≥ DataSub_MRR_Threshold else 0

XAPI_Fee
  • Unit: EUR per month
  • Default: 5000 EUR
  • Description: Monthly fee for external API service (e.g., broker integration API).

XAPI_MRR_Threshold
  • Unit: EUR
  • Default: 15000 EUR
  • Description: MRR threshold above which the API service is activated.
  • Formula where used: XAPI_Cost = XAPI_Fee if MRR ≥ XAPI_MRR_Threshold else 0

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1.9 BROKER-RELATED PARAMETERS (Informational)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Broker_MinCapital_Low, Broker_MinCapital_High, Broker_TargetCapital
  • Unit: EUR
  • Defaults: 150,000, 750,000, 2,000,000 EUR
  • Description: Minimum capital requirements and targets for broker
    integration/partnership. These are informational parameters not used
    in current calculations.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1.10 FOLLOWER ADS PARAMETERS (Future Implementation)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

FollowerAds_CPM_EUR
  • Unit: EUR per 1000 impressions
  • Default: 7 EUR

FollowerAds_Reach_to_Follower_Rate
  • Unit: Decimal (0-1)
  • Default: 0.1 (10%)

FollowerAds_Budget_Y1, FollowerAds_Budget_Y2, FollowerAds_Budget_Y3
  • Unit: EUR per month
  • Defaults: 500, 750, 1000 EUR
  • Description: Budget for paid ads targeting followers (boost posts).
  • Note: Currently NOT implemented in calculations.


================================================================================
SECTION 2: MONTHLY MODEL CALCULATIONS
================================================================================

The monthly model tracks 36 months (3 years) of operations with detailed
month-by-month calculations. Below are all calculated columns and their formulas.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.1 TIME TRACKING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Year
  • Values: 1, 2, 3
  • Description: Calendar year number

Month
  • Values: 1-12 (repeats for each year)
  • Description: Month number within the year

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.2 FOLLOWER GROWTH MECHANICS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Followers_Start
  • Formula: Previous month's Followers_End (or Followers_0 for Month 1)
  • Description: Number of social media followers at the start of the month

Followers_End
  • Formula: Followers_Start × (1 + Follower_Monthly_Growth)
  • Description: Number of followers at month end after organic growth
  • Example: 1000 × (1 + 0.08) = 1080 followers

Posts
  • Formula: Posts_per_Month_Y1/Y2/Y3 (depends on current year)
  • Description: Number of posts published this month

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.3 SOCIAL MEDIA IMPRESSIONS AND REACH
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Impr_Followers (Impressions to Followers)
  • Formula: ((Followers_Start + Followers_End) / 2) × Posts × Reach_per_Post × Frequency_Impressions_per_User
  • Description: Total impressions generated among existing followers
  • Logic: 
    - Use average followers during the month
    - Each post reaches a percentage of followers
    - Multiply by frequency (users see content multiple times)
  • Example: ((1000 + 1080) / 2) × 120 × 0.04 × 1.5 = 7488 impressions

Impr_NonFollowers (Impressions to Non-Followers)
  • Formula: Impr_Followers × NonFollower_Reach_Multiplier
  • Description: Impressions generated among non-followers through platform
    algorithms (explore page, hashtags, shares)
  • Example: 7488 × 0.5 = 3744 impressions

Social_Views (Total Social Views)
  • Formula: Impr_Followers + Impr_NonFollowers
  • Description: Total content impressions across all users
  • Example: 7488 + 3744 = 11,232 views

NewUnique_NonFollowers (New Unique Non-Followers Reached)
  • Formula: Impr_NonFollowers / Frequency_Impressions_per_User
  • Description: Number of unique non-followers who saw your content,
    accounting for frequency (same user seeing multiple times)
  • Example: 3744 / 1.5 = 2496 unique users

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.4 WEBSITE TRAFFIC BY CHANNEL
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Org_Visitors (Organic Social Visitors)
  • Formula: NewUnique_NonFollowers × Organic_CTR_to_Site
  • Description: Website visitors from organic social media content
  • Example: 2496 × 0.015 = 37.44 visitors

Inf_Visitors (Influencer Visitors)
  • Formula: Inf_Collabs_Y1/Y2/Y3 × Inf_Visitors_per_Collab
  • Description: Website visitors from influencer marketing campaigns
  • Example: 1 × 6000 = 6000 visitors

Other_Visitors (Other Channel Visitors)
  • Formula: Other_Marketing_Budget_Y1/Y2/Y3 / 2.0
  • Description: Website visitors from other marketing channels
  • Assumption: Effective CPC (Cost Per Click) of $2
  • Example: 200 / 2 = 100 visitors

Visitors_Total (Total Website Visitors)
  • Formula: Org_Visitors + Inf_Visitors + Other_Visitors
  • Description: Total unique website visitors from all channels
  • Example: 37.44 + 6000 + 100 = 6137.44 visitors

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.5 CONVERSION FUNNEL
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Signups (Total Signups)
  • Formula: Visitors_Total × ConvVS
  • Description: Users who create an account (free signup)
  • Example: 6137.44 × 0.13 = 797.87 signups

Channel-Specific Signups:
  The total signups are proportionally attributed to each traffic channel:

Org_Signups (Organic Social Signups)
  • Formula: Signups × (Org_Visitors / Visitors_Total)
  • Description: Signups attributed to organic social traffic
  • Example: 797.87 × (37.44 / 6137.44) = 4.87 signups

Inf_Signups (Influencer Signups)
  • Formula: Signups × (Inf_Visitors / Visitors_Total)
  • Description: Signups attributed to influencer traffic
  • Example: 797.87 × (6000 / 6137.44) = 780 signups

Other_Signups (Other Channel Signups)
  • Formula: Signups × (Other_Visitors / Visitors_Total)
  • Description: Signups attributed to other marketing channels
  • Example: 797.87 × (100 / 6137.44) = 13 signups

Channel-Specific New Payers:
  Each channel's signups convert to paying users at the ConvSP rate:

Org_New_Payers (Organic New Paying Users)
  • Formula: Org_Signups × ConvSP
  • Description: New paying customers from organic social
  • Example: 4.87 × 0.035 = 0.17 new payers

Inf_New_Payers (Influencer New Paying Users)
  • Formula: Inf_Signups × ConvSP
  • Description: New paying customers from influencer campaigns
  • Example: 780 × 0.035 = 27.3 new payers

Other_New_Payers (Other Channel New Paying Users)
  • Formula: Other_Signups × ConvSP
  • Description: New paying customers from other channels
  • Example: 13 × 0.035 = 0.455 new payers

Referral_New_Payers (Referral New Paying Users)
  • Formula: Paying_Users_Start × Referral_Monthly_Rate
  • Description: New paying customers from existing user referrals
  • Example: 0 × 0.02 = 0 (Month 1 has no existing users)

New_Paying_Users (Total New Paying Users)
  • Formula: Org_New_Payers + Inf_New_Payers + Other_New_Payers + Referral_New_Payers
  • Description: Total new customers acquired this month across all channels
  • Example: 0.17 + 27.3 + 0.455 + 0 = 27.925 new payers

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.6 USER COHORT DYNAMICS (RETENTION)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Churn_Rate
  • Formula: ChurnY1/Y2/Y3 (depends on current year)
  • Description: Monthly churn rate for current year

Paying_Users_Start
  • Formula: Previous month's Paying_Users_End (or 0 for Month 1)
  • Description: Number of paying subscribers at month start

Churned_Users
  • Formula: Paying_Users_Start × Churn_Rate
  • Description: Number of paying users who cancelled this month
  • Example: 0 × 0.06 = 0 (Month 1 has no one to churn)

Paying_Users_End
  • Formula: Paying_Users_Start - Churned_Users + New_Paying_Users
  • Description: Number of paying subscribers at month end
  • Logic: Start with existing users, subtract cancellations, add new customers
  • Example: 0 - 0 + 27.925 = 27.925 paying users

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.7 REVENUE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ARPU (Average Revenue Per User)
  • Formula: ARPU parameter from assumptions
  • Description: Monthly revenue per paying user
  • Example: 20 EUR

MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue)
  • Formula: Paying_Users_End × ARPU
  • Description: Total monthly subscription revenue
  • Example: 27.925 × 20 = 558.51 EUR

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.8 MARKETING COSTS BY CHANNEL
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Org_Marketing_Spend (Organic Social Marketing Spend)
  • Formula: Posts × Org_Cost_per_Post
  • Description: Cost of creating organic social content
  • Example: 120 × 1 = 120 EUR

Inf_Marketing_Spend (Influencer Marketing Spend)
  • Formula: Inf_New_Payers × Influencer_Reward_per_Sub
  • Description: Commission paid to influencers (performance-based)
  • Example: 27.3 × 10 = 273 EUR

Other_Marketing_Spend (Other Channel Marketing Spend)
  • Formula: Other_Marketing_Budget_Y1/Y2/Y3
  • Description: Budget spent on other marketing channels
  • Example: 200 EUR

Referral_Marketing_Spend (Referral Program Spend)
  • Formula: Referral_New_Payers × Referral_Reward_per_Sub
  • Description: Rewards paid to existing users for referrals
  • Example: 0 × 10 = 0 EUR

Total_Marketing_Spend (Total Marketing Spend)
  • Formula: Org_Marketing_Spend + Inf_Marketing_Spend + Other_Marketing_Spend + Referral_Marketing_Spend
  • Description: Total marketing expenses for the month
  • Example: 120 + 273 + 200 + 0 = 593 EUR

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.9 OPERATIONAL COSTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

DataSub_Cost (Data Subscription Cost)
  • Formula: DataSub_Fee if MRR ≥ DataSub_MRR_Threshold, else 0
  • Description: Monthly data provider subscription cost (activated when
    revenue exceeds threshold)
  • Example: 0 EUR (558.51 < 5000 threshold)

XAPI_Cost (External API Cost)
  • Formula: XAPI_Fee if MRR ≥ XAPI_MRR_Threshold, else 0
  • Description: Monthly external API subscription cost (activated when
    revenue exceeds threshold)
  • Example: 0 EUR (558.51 < 15000 threshold)

Base_Fixed_Cost (Base Fixed Operational Costs)
  • Formula: BaseFixedCost parameter
  • Description: Fixed monthly costs (salaries, office, etc.)
  • Example: 1000 EUR

Total_Costs (Total Monthly Costs)
  • Formula: Total_Marketing_Spend + DataSub_Cost + XAPI_Cost + Base_Fixed_Cost
  • Description: All monthly expenses combined
  • Example: 593 + 0 + 0 + 1000 = 1593 EUR

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.10 CASH FLOW
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Net_Cash_Flow (Net Monthly Cash Flow)
  • Formula: MRR - Total_Costs
  • Description: Monthly profit/loss (positive = profit, negative = burn)
  • Example: 558.51 - 1593 = -1034.49 EUR (burning cash in Month 1)

Cumulative_Cash (Cumulative Cash Position)
  • Formula: Previous month's Cumulative_Cash + Net_Cash_Flow
  • Description: Running total of cash position (assuming no external funding)
  • Logic: Shows total cash burned or generated since Month 1
  • Example Month 1: 0 + (-1034.49) = -1034.49 EUR
  • Example Month 2: -1034.49 + (-523.51) = -1558 EUR

SECTION 3: YEARLY SUMMARY CALCULATIONS
================================================================================

The yearly summary aggregates monthly data into annual metrics for each of
the 3 years. These metrics are used to evaluate annual performance and
calculate key SaaS metrics.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.1 BASIC METRICS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Year
  • Values: 1, 2, 3
  • Description: Year number

End_Paying_Users
  • Formula: Last month of year: Paying_Users_End
  • Description: Number of paying subscribers at end of year
  • Example Y1: 271 users (from Month 12 of Year 1)

End_MRR_EUR
  • Formula: Last month of year: MRR
  • Description: Monthly recurring revenue at year end
  • Example Y1: 5429 EUR

ARR_EUR (Annual Recurring Revenue)
  • Formula: End_MRR_EUR × 12
  • Description: Annualized revenue based on year-end MRR
  • Example Y1: 5429 × 12 = 65,145 EUR
  • Note: This is a forward-looking metric (projected annual revenue if
    current MRR is maintained)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.2 CUSTOMER ACQUISITION METRICS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Total_New_Customers
  • Formula: SUM(New_Paying_Users) for all 12 months in year
  • Description: Total new paying customers acquired during the year
  • Example Y1: 369 customers

Org_New_Payers (Organic Channel New Payers - Annual)
  • Formula: SUM(Org_New_Payers) for all 12 months
  • Description: Total new payers from organic social this year

Inf_New_Payers (Influencer Channel New Payers - Annual)
  • Formula: SUM(Inf_New_Payers) for all 12 months
  • Description: Total new payers from influencer campaigns this year

Other_New_Payers (Other Channel New Payers - Annual)
  • Formula: SUM(Other_New_Payers) for all 12 months
  • Description: Total new payers from other marketing this year

Referral_New_Payers (Referral New Payers - Annual)
  • Formula: SUM(Referral_New_Payers) for all 12 months
  • Description: Total new payers from referrals this year

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.3 MARKETING SPEND METRICS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Org_Marketing_Spend_EUR (Organic Marketing - Annual)
  • Formula: SUM(Org_Marketing_Spend) for all 12 months
  • Description: Total spent on organic social content this year

Inf_Marketing_Spend_EUR (Influencer Marketing - Annual)
  • Formula: SUM(Inf_Marketing_Spend) for all 12 months
  • Description: Total spent on influencer commissions this year

Other_Marketing_Spend_EUR (Other Marketing - Annual)
  • Formula: SUM(Other_Marketing_Spend) for all 12 months
  • Description: Total spent on other marketing channels this year

Referral_Marketing_Spend_EUR (Referral Program - Annual)
  • Formula: SUM(Referral_Marketing_Spend) for all 12 months
  • Description: Total spent on referral rewards this year

Total_Marketing_Spend_EUR (Total Marketing - Annual)
  • Formula: Org_Marketing_Spend_EUR + Inf_Marketing_Spend_EUR + Other_Marketing_Spend_EUR + Referral_Marketing_Spend_EUR
  • Description: Total marketing investment for the year

Average_CAC_EUR (Average Customer Acquisition Cost)
  • Formula: Total_Marketing_Spend_EUR / Total_New_Customers
  • Description: Average cost to acquire one paying customer this year
  • Example Y1: 7440 / 369 = 20.18 EUR per customer
  • Benchmark: Lower is better; compare to LTV for unit economics health

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.4 RETENTION AND LIFETIME VALUE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Assumed_Monthly_Churn
  • Formula: ChurnY1/Y2/Y3 (assumed churn rate for the year)
  • Description: The monthly churn rate parameter used for this year

LTV_EUR (Customer Lifetime Value)
  • Formula: (ARPU × GrossMargin) / Assumed_Monthly_Churn
  • Description: Expected total profit from one customer over their lifetime
  • Logic: 
    - Gross profit per month = ARPU × GrossMargin
    - Average customer lifetime = 1 / Monthly_Churn
    - LTV = Monthly_Profit / Churn_Rate
  • Example Y1: (20 × 0.8) / 0.06 = 266.67 EUR
  • Benchmark: Industry standard is LTV > 3 × CAC

LTV_CAC_Ratio (LTV to CAC Ratio)
  • Formula: LTV_EUR / Average_CAC_EUR
  • Description: How many times the customer lifetime value exceeds acquisition cost
  • Example Y1: 266.67 / 20.18 = 13.22x
  • Interpretation:
    - < 1.0: Losing money on each customer (unsustainable)
    - 1.0 - 3.0: Breaking even or low margin
    - 3.0+: Healthy SaaS economics
    - 5.0+: Excellent unit economics
  • Note: This is a key metric for investor evaluation and business health

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.5 FINANCIAL METRICS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Cumulative_Cash_EndOfYear
  • Formula: Last month of year: Cumulative_Cash
  • Description: Total cumulative cash position at year end
  • Example Y1: 14,417 EUR (cumulative profit after 12 months)
  • Interpretation:
    - Negative: Total cash burned since start (needs funding)
    - Positive: Net cash generated (self-sustainable)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.6 TRAFFIC AND ENGAGEMENT METRICS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Total_Org_Visitors (Total Organic Visitors - Annual)
  • Formula: SUM(Org_Visitors) for all 12 months
  • Description: Total website visitors from organic social this year

Total_Inf_Visitors (Total Influencer Visitors - Annual)
  • Formula: SUM(Inf_Visitors) for all 12 months
  • Description: Total website visitors from influencers this year

Total_Other_Visitors (Total Other Visitors - Annual)
  • Formula: SUM(Other_Visitors) for all 12 months
  • Description: Total website visitors from other channels this year

Total_Visitors (Total Website Visitors - Annual)
  • Formula: Total_Org_Visitors + Total_Inf_Visitors + Total_Other_Visitors
  • Description: Total website traffic for the year

Share_Org_Visitors (Organic Visitor Share)
  • Formula: Total_Org_Visitors / Total_Visitors
  • Description: Percentage of traffic from organic social
  • Example Y1: 711 / 73,911 = 0.96% (0.01)

Share_Inf_Visitors (Influencer Visitor Share)
  • Formula: Total_Inf_Visitors / Total_Visitors
  • Description: Percentage of traffic from influencer marketing
  • Example Y1: 72,000 / 73,911 = 97.4%

Share_Other_Visitors (Other Channel Visitor Share)
  • Formula: Total_Other_Visitors / Total_Visitors
  • Description: Percentage of traffic from other marketing channels
  • Example Y1: 1,200 / 73,911 = 1.6%

Total_Social_Views (Total Social Media Views - Annual)
  • Formula: SUM(Social_Views) for all 12 months
  • Description: Total social media impressions generated this year

End_Followers (Year-End Followers)
  • Formula: Last month of year: Followers_End
  • Description: Social media followers at year end


================================================================================
SECTION 4: KEY BUSINESS LOGIC AND ASSUMPTIONS
================================================================================

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4.1 FOLLOWER GROWTH MODEL
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The model assumes compound monthly growth of social media followers:
  • Growth is organic (not paid follower acquisition)
  • Growth rate is constant each month (8% in default)
  • Formula: Followers(month) = Followers(month-1) × 1.08
  • Over 36 months: 1,000 → 15,968 followers (15.97x growth)

This assumes:
  • Consistent content quality and posting frequency
  • Algorithmic amplification continues
  • No major platform changes or audience saturation

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4.2 SOCIAL MEDIA REACH MODEL
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Impressions are calculated using:
  1. Organic reach per post (4% of followers see each post)
  2. Frequency (users see content 1.5 times on average)
  3. Non-follower amplification (50% extra reach beyond followers)

This models typical social media dynamics where:
  • Platforms limit organic reach to incentivize paid ads
  • Good content gets shared/recommended to non-followers
  • Users see brands multiple times through different posts/placements

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4.3 CONVERSION FUNNEL ASSUMPTIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Two-step conversion funnel:
  1. Visitor → Signup (13% conversion)
  2. Signup → Paying (3.5% conversion)
  3. Combined: Visitor → Paying = 0.13 × 0.035 = 0.455%

This means for every 1000 visitors:
  • 130 sign up for free accounts
  • 4.55 convert to paying customers

These rates are:
  • Visitor→Signup: Industry average for SaaS (10-15%)
  • Signup→Paying: Typical for freemium models (2-5%)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4.4 MULTI-CHANNEL ATTRIBUTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The model attributes customers to channels based on traffic proportion:
  • If influencers drive 80% of traffic, they get credit for 80% of signups
  • This is "last-touch attribution" simplified

Limitations:
  • Doesn't account for multi-touch (user sees influencer + organic)
  • Doesn't account for assisted conversions
  • Real world: some channels assist, others close the deal

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4.5 CHURN AND RETENTION MODEL
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Monthly churn model:
  • Churn happens at start of month (users who don't renew)
  • New users acquired during month don't churn same month
  • Churn improves over years as product matures:
    - Year 1: 6% monthly (50% annual churn)
    - Year 2: 5.5% monthly (46% annual)
    - Year 3: 5% monthly (43% annual)

Customer lifetime calculation:
  • Average lifetime (months) = 1 / monthly_churn
  • 6% churn = 16.7 month average lifetime
  • 5% churn = 20 month average lifetime

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4.6 COST THRESHOLD MODEL
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Certain costs activate only when revenue crosses thresholds:
  • Data subscription: Activated at 5,000 EUR MRR
  • External API: Activated at 15,000 EUR MRR

This models:
  • Free tiers available at low scale
  • Enterprise pricing kicks in as you grow
  • Cost structure changes with scale

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4.7 CASH FLOW MODEL
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Cash flow is calculated simply as:
  • Cash In: MRR (assume all revenue collected monthly)
  • Cash Out: All costs paid monthly
  • Net: MRR - Costs

Simplifications:
  • No accounts receivable (instant payment)
  • No deferred revenue accounting
  • No payment processing fees
  • No taxes
  • No debt or equity financing

This shows "unit economics" and operating leverage, not full financial statements.

SECTION 5: INTERPRETING THE MODEL RESULTS
================================================================================

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.1 WHAT TO LOOK FOR IN MONTHLY DATA
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Early Months (1-6):
  • Expect negative cash flow (burning cash to acquire customers)
  • Marketing spend > revenue
  • Focus on CAC and conversion rates

Growth Phase (7-18):
  • Cash flow improving but still may be negative
  • User base compounding (new + retained)
  • Watch churn rate - retention becomes critical

Maturity (19-36):
  • Should reach cash flow positive
  • Cumulative cash turns positive (break-even point)
  • Unit economics proven at scale

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.2 KEY METRICS TO MONITOR
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)
   • Lower is better
   • Should decrease over time (efficiency improvements)
   • Compare across channels to optimize budget

2. LTV (Lifetime Value)
   • Higher is better
   • Improves as churn decreases
   • Must be > 3x CAC for healthy business

3. LTV/CAC Ratio
   • Minimum: 3.0x (breakeven unit economics)
   • Good: 5.0x+ (strong business model)
   • Excellent: 8.0x+ (capital efficient growth)

4. Payback Period (not shown but calculable)
   • Months to recover CAC from gross margin
   • Formula: CAC / (ARPU × Gross Margin)
   • Good: < 12 months
   • Acceptable: 12-18 months

5. Monthly Churn Rate
   • Good: < 5%
   • Acceptable: 5-7%
   • Problem: > 7%

6. Net Cash Flow
   • When does it turn positive?
   • Month of profitability is key milestone

7. Cumulative Cash
   • Total capital needed to reach breakeven
   • Shows funding requirements

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.3 SCENARIO ANALYSIS RECOMMENDATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Use the model to test scenarios:

1. Optimistic Case:
   • Lower churn (-1%)
   • Higher conversion rates (+25%)
   • More influencer collabs (+1 per month)

2. Pessimistic Case:
   • Higher churn (+1%)
   • Lower conversion rates (-25%)
   • Higher CAC (+50%)

3. Different Strategies:
   • Organic-focused: Cut influencer budget, increase posting
   • Paid-focused: Increase other marketing budget
   • Premium pricing: Increase ARPU to 30-40 EUR

4. Sensitivity Analysis:
   • Which parameter changes have biggest impact?
   • What's the minimum viable conversion rate?
   • How much can churn increase before model breaks?


================================================================================
SECTION 6: LIMITATIONS AND ASSUMPTIONS
================================================================================

This model is a simplified representation. Real-world considerations:

1. Model doesn't include:
   • Taxes (VAT, corporate income tax)
   • Payment processing fees (2-3% of revenue)
   • Multiple pricing tiers (only average ARPU)
   • Seasonal variations
   • Competitor actions
   • Platform algorithm changes
   • Cohort-based retention (all users have same churn)

2. Simplifying assumptions:
   • Linear relationships (real world has diminishing returns)
   • Constant conversion rates (actually vary by source, seasonality)
   • Instant payment collection (no payment delays)
   • No failed payments or fraud
   • No team scaling costs (assumes costs grow linearly)

3. Use this model for:
   • Strategic planning and goal setting
   • Understanding unit economics
   • Fundraising projections
   • Channel budget optimization
   • Sensitivity analysis

4. Do NOT use for:
   • Precise financial accounting
   • Tax planning
   • Guaranteeing specific results
   • Short-term (weekly) forecasting

SECTION 7: HOW TO USE THIS MODEL EFFECTIVELY
================================================================================

1. Start with Benchmarks:
   • Research industry benchmarks for your SaaS vertical
   • Input realistic conversion rates (test with real data)
   • Use conservative estimates for new assumptions

2. Validate with Real Data:
   • Once you have actual performance data, update assumptions
   • Compare monthly actuals vs. forecast
   • Adjust model parameters based on learnings

3. Regular Updates:
   • Review monthly and update assumptions
   • Track variance (actual vs. forecast)
   • Identify which assumptions were wrong

4. Scenario Planning:
   • Always maintain optimistic/base/pessimistic cases
   • Understand which metrics are most sensitive
   • Plan for multiple futures

5. Focus on Key Drivers:
   • Not all parameters matter equally
   • Identify your 3-5 key drivers (usually: CAC, conversion, churn, ARPU)
   • Obsess over improving those specific metrics
